Test-Taking Strategy

⏱ Time Management

  • R&W: ~71 sec per question. Flag hard ones, return if time allows.
  • Math: ~95 sec per question. Don't get stuck — flag and move.
  • Check time at question 10 and question 20 in each module.
  • Never leave a blank — no penalty for guessing.

🎯 Module 1 Priority

  • Accuracy in Module 1 determines your Module 2 difficulty tier.
  • Aim for 85%+ correct in Module 1 to unlock the hard module.
  • Don't rush early questions — these are your most valuable points.
  • Hard Module 2 = higher score ceiling. Embrace the difficulty.

✂️ Process of Elimination

  • Cross out clearly wrong answers first — usually 1–2 per question.
  • On R&W: eliminate answers that add info not in the passage.
  • On Math: plug in numbers to test answer choices when stuck.
  • When down to 2, read both very carefully — one will be subtly wrong.

🔢 Desmos Strategy

  • Graph systems of equations — intersection = solution.
  • Use it to check algebra answers, not replace thinking.
  • Visualize parabolas, find vertex or roots instantly.
  • Practice with Desmos before test day — it's the same tool.

⚠️ Common Careless Mistakes

  • Misreading "NOT" or "BEST" — underline key words.
  • Sign errors in algebra — check negative distribution.
  • Units — does your answer make sense in context?
  • Answering what you thought was asked, not what was asked.

📖 R&W Passage Approach

  • Read the question stem first — know what you're hunting for.
  • For vocab-in-context: try each answer in the blank, choose best fit.
  • For grammar: read aloud mentally — your ear catches errors.
  • For transitions: identify the relationship between ideas first.

Mindset & Psychology

"A wrong answer is data, not failure. Every mistake tells you exactly what to study next."
— The top scorer's mindset
"The SAT is not an IQ test. It tests learned skills — and skills can be taught, practiced, and improved."
— College Board's own framing
🧠
Managing Test Anxiety
  • Before the test: box breathing (4 in, hold 4, out 4, hold 4) — resets your nervous system.
  • During hard questions: remind yourself that hard = harder module = good sign.
  • If you spiral: refocus on the current question only. Forget the last one.
  • Anxiety is energy — channel it into focus, not panic.
  • Confidence comes from preparation. Every practice session builds it.
📈
Building Study Momentum
  • 30 consistent minutes beats a 3-hour cram session. Every time.
  • Track your error patterns weekly — you'll see real progress.
  • Celebrate small wins: mastering one topic is a genuine improvement.
  • Take one full day off each week. Recovery is part of performance.
  • Retakes are normal. Most students improve 50–150 points between attempts.

Day-Of Playbook

🌙
The Night Before
  • Pack your bag: admission ticket, photo ID, approved calculator, pencils/pens, snacks, water
  • Confirm test center location and travel time
  • No new studying tonight — your brain needs consolidation, not cramming
  • Light review only: glance at your error log and key formulas (10 min max)
  • Be in bed by 10pm — 8 hours of sleep is more valuable than any last-minute review
  • Lay out your clothes so there are zero decisions to make in the morning
☀️
Morning Of
  • Eat a real breakfast: protein + complex carbs (eggs + oatmeal, not sugary cereal)
  • Do a 5–10 min light warmup: 5 easy math questions or 5 grammar questions
  • Hydrate — bring water to the test center
  • Leave 30 minutes early — arriving stressed costs you points
  • Box breathing on the way: 4-count in, 4-count hold, 4-count out
✏️
During the Test
  • Read every question carefully — underline key words like "NOT," "BEST," "EXCEPT"
  • Flag hard questions and move on — never spend more than 2 min stuck
  • Never leave a blank — there is NO penalty for guessing
  • Time check at Q10 and Q20 in each module
  • After each module: close your eyes, take 3 deep breaths, reset mentally
  • If Module 2 feels harder — celebrate! You earned the hard module.
✅
After the Test
  • Don't stress about questions you think you missed — you can't change them now
  • Write down what surprised you while memory is fresh — gold for retake prep
  • Scores arrive in ~2 weeks — check total AND section breakdowns
  • Retaking? Most students gain 50–150 points. You know exactly what to improve.
  • Celebrate the effort — regardless of score, you put in real work.
